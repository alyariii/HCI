Topic: General Knowledge Reviewer on Science, Society, and Digital Literacy

1. Energy and Environment

  • Energy is the capacity to do work.
  • Forms of energy include:
    • heat
    • light
    • chemical
    • mechanical
    • electrical
  • A cellphone battery stores chemical energy that can be converted into electrical energy.
  • Renewable energy sources include:
    • solar
    • wind
    • hydropower
  • Coal and oil are nonrenewable because they take millions of years to form.
  • Weather refers to short-term atmospheric conditions.
  • Climate refers to long-term atmospheric patterns.
  • The greenhouse effect is a natural process that traps some of the Sun’s heat.
  • Too many greenhouse gases can intensify warming.
  • The water cycle includes:
    • evaporation
    • condensation
    • precipitation

2. Biology and Health

  • Cells are the basic unit of life.
  • Photosynthesis uses sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide to produce glucose and oxygen.
  • Cellular respiration breaks down glucose to release usable energy.
  • Vaccines help the immune system recognize pathogens.
  • Antibiotics treat bacterial infections.
  • Misuse of antibiotics may contribute to antibiotic resistance.
  • The circulatory system transports oxygen, nutrients, and wastes.
  • The human heart has four chambers.

3. Earth Science

  • Earth’s outer layer is divided into tectonic plates.
  • Plate movement may cause:
    • earthquakes
    • volcanic eruptions
    • mountain formation
  • The focus is the point inside Earth where an earthquake begins.
  • The epicenter is the point on the surface directly above the focus.
  • Seismic waves carry earthquake energy.
  • Seismographs record earthquake activity.

4. Economics and Society

  • Scarcity means resources are limited but wants are many.
  • Because of scarcity, people and societies must make choices.
  • Opportunity cost is the value of the next best alternative given up.
  • Inflation is the general increase in prices over time.
  • Inflation reduces the purchasing power of money.
  • Demand is the quantity consumers are willing and able to buy.
  • Supply is the quantity producers are willing and able to sell.

5. Government and Digital Literacy

  • The Philippines has three branches of government:
    • Executive
    • Legislative
    • Judicial
  • The executive implements laws.
  • The legislative makes laws.
  • The judicial interprets laws.
  • The Constitution is the supreme law of the land.
  • Separation of powers distributes authority.
  • Checks and balances allow one branch to limit another.
  • The internet is a global network of connected computer systems.
  • The World Wide Web is a service that runs on the internet.
  • Browsers are used to access websites.
  • HTTP transfers web content.
  • HTTPS is more secure because it uses encryption.
  • Good digital safety includes:
    • strong passwords
    • multi-factor authentication
    • checking credibility, evidence, and bias
