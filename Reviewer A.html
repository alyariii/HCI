1. Energy and Environment

Energy is the capacity to do work and appears in forms such as heat, light, chemical, mechanical, and electrical energy. Energy transformations happen in daily life, such as when a cellphone battery stores chemical energy that is converted into electrical energy. Renewable energy resources, such as solar, wind, and hydropower, can be replenished naturally, while fossil fuels like coal and oil take millions of years to form and are considered nonrenewable.

Weather describes short-term atmospheric conditions in a specific place and time, while climate refers to long-term patterns observed over many years. The greenhouse effect is a natural process in which gases in the atmosphere trap some of the Sun’s heat, helping maintain temperatures suitable for life. However, increasing greenhouse gases can intensify warming. The water cycle continuously moves water through evaporation, condensation, and precipitation.

2. Biology and Health

Cells are the basic unit of life. In plants, photosynthesis uses sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide to produce glucose and oxygen. In both plants and animals, cellular respiration breaks down glucose to release usable energy. Vaccines prepare the immune system to recognize and respond to pathogens, while antibiotics are specifically used to treat bacterial infections. Misuse of antibiotics may contribute to antibiotic resistance. The circulatory system transports oxygen, nutrients, and wastes throughout the body, and the human heart has four chambers.

3. Earth Science

Earth’s outer layer is divided into tectonic plates that move slowly over time. Their movement may cause earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, and mountain formation. When an earthquake occurs, the focus is the point inside Earth where it begins, while the epicenter is the point on the surface directly above the focus. Seismic waves carry the energy released by earthquakes, and instruments such as seismographs help record this activity.

4. Economics and Society

Scarcity exists because resources are limited while human wants are many. Because of scarcity, individuals and societies must make choices. Opportunity cost refers to the value of the next best alternative that is given up. Inflation is the general increase in prices over time, reducing the purchasing power of money. Demand refers to the quantity consumers are willing and able to buy, while supply refers to the quantity producers are willing and able to sell.

5. Government and Digital Literacy

The Philippines follows a democratic system with three branches of government: executive, legislative, and judicial. The executive branch implements laws, the legislative branch makes laws, and the judicial branch interprets laws. The Constitution is the supreme law of the land. Separation of powers helps distribute authority among branches, while checks and balances allow one branch to limit the actions of another.

In digital literacy, the internet is the large global system of connected computer networks, while the World Wide Web is a service that allows users to access linked pages and resources. Browsers are used to open websites. HTTP is a protocol used for transferring web content, while HTTPS is more secure because it includes encryption. Good digital safety practices include using strong passwords, enabling multi-factor authentication, and evaluating online information for credibility, evidence, and possible bias.
